Under the hood

Built to keep your recordings yours.

Private by design

No uploads, no accounts, no cloud. Every second of audio is processed on your machine and never leaves it — safe for student voices, oral exams, and IEP recordings.

Word-level timestamps

A timestamp for every word, not just every line. Perfect for captions, pulling clips, and jumping straight to the moment that matters.

Metal-accelerated

Powered by whisper.cpp (large-v3) with Metal GPU acceleration, so a full class period transcribes in minutes, not hours.

Audio and video

Drop in a recording or a video. tscribe converts video to 16 kHz mono with AVFoundation, then transcribes either one.

Getting started

Install in about a minute.

  1. Open the downloaded .dmg and drag tscribe to your Applications folder.
  2. First launch — Control-click, don't double-click. Because tscribe is open source and not notarized by Apple, a normal double-click shows "tscribe cannot be opened because it is from an unidentified developer." Instead, Control-click (or right-click) tscribe in Applications, choose Open, then click Open in the dialog. You only do this once.
  3. Still blocked? Open it anyway. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll to Security, and click Open Anyway next to the tscribe message — then confirm with Touch ID or your password.
  4. Let it finish setting up. The first time you open the Standard edition, tscribe downloads its speech model (~2.9 GB) once — keep your Mac online for that first run. Every transcription after that is fully offline. (The Complete edition skips this — the model is already inside.)

Why the warning? tscribe isn't code-signed with a paid Apple Developer certificate, so macOS Gatekeeper asks before the first launch. The full source is on GitHub — you can read exactly what it does before you run it.
